- Investing risk tolerance is your comfort level with uncertainty in your investments. Think about how you might feel if the stock market dropped 10% in a week or if the value of your investing portfolio bounces around when the markets fluctuate. Some investors can watch their account balance dip without worry. Others get anxious and want to sell everything.

Risk tolerance matters because it helps to shape your investment strategy. If you pick investments that are too risky for your comfort level, you might panic and sell at the wrong time. If you play it too safe, you might not earn enough money to reach your financial goals. The sweet spot is to find investments you can stick with through both good markets and rough patches.
