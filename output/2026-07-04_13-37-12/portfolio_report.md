## Types of Risk Tolerance

### Conservative Risk Tolerance

Investments are solely focused on not losing capital. A conservative risk investment portfolio has a larger portion of low-risk, fixed income investments and a lower portion of high-risk stocks and funds that are likely to garner higher returns.

### Moderate Risk Tolerance [...] ### Moderate Risk Tolerance

A moderate risk investment portfolio can have both conservative and aggressive investments. It is traditionally a 60/40 split between stocks and bonds, where some capital is invested for growth, and some is invested for stability.

### Aggressive Risk Tolerance

An aggressive risk tolerance portfolio has a majority of investments in volatile instruments that do not have a guaranteed return, like real estate and stocks. These types of investments, while not guaranteed to make a profit or return, generally have a higher return when they do. Generally, aggressive investors reach for maximum return with maximum risk.
