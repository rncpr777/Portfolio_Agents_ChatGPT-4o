## Types of Risk Tolerance

### Conservative Risk Tolerance

Investments are solely focused on not losing capital. A conservative risk investment portfolio has a larger portion of low-risk, fixed income investments and a lower portion of high-risk stocks and funds that are likely to garner higher returns.

A moderate risk investment portfolio can have both conservative and aggressive investments. It is traditionally a 60/40 split between stocks and bonds, where some capital is invested for growth, and some is invested for stability.

### Aggressive Risk Tolerance

An aggressive risk tolerance portfolio has a majority of investments in volatile instruments that do not have a guaranteed return, like real estate and stocks. ## Risk Tolerance vs. Risk Appetite

Risk appetite is a broader term that refers to the levels of risk that are considered acceptable on a higher level. It is the general level of risk an organization accepts before deciding to take action to reduce the risk. 

For example, your risk appetite regarding the purchase of new shoes could be described as what price you think you’ll have to pay for the shoes.
